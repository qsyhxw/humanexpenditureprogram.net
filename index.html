Endings & Choices Guide

Human Expenditure Program is a browser game about trade-offs. Your choices ripple through the day and lock in different outcomes. Below is a spoiler-light overview to help you explore without ruining the surprise.

1) Compliance Ending

Keep metrics stable, follow instructions, avoid risky actions. You’ll likely reach a “stable but hollow” resolution. It’s the easiest path, but it hides much of the game’s moral texture.

  • Prioritize safety and routine.
  • Minimize conflicts and deviations.
  • Great for a first run to learn the systems.

2) Defiance Ending

Push back on directives, experiment with boundaries, and accept short-term instability. This route surfaces hidden scenes and sharp consequences. Expect friction and a bittersweet finale.

  • Break patterns to reveal new prompts.
  • Accept the cost of non-compliance.
  • Best if you want stronger narrative payoff.

3) Optimization Ending

Master the resource loop: timing, thresholds, and small efficiencies. Done right, you can stabilize while still unlocking optional scenes—a more nuanced “win” that rewards planning.

  • Track thresholds; tiny margins matter.
  • Sequence actions to prevent cascading failures.
  • Ideal for players who enjoy systems mastery.

4) Hidden/Variant Endings

Special outcomes may require unusual orders, timing windows, or repeated runs. Some scenes only appear after you’ve seen another ending first. If something feels “off,” you’re probably on the right track.

  • Revisit choices after a completed run.
  • Try extreme strategies to reveal flags.
  • Keep notes—memory is part of the design.

Tips (spoiler-light)

  • Use Fullscreen for better visibility (button, double-click, or press F).
  • Short sessions are fine; treat early runs as scouting.
  • When in doubt, try the opposite of what “feels safe.”
